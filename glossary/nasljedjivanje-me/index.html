Nasljeđivanje Nekretnine

Nasljeđivanje nekretnine refers to property inheritance in Montenegro — the legal process by which real estate ownership transfers to heirs upon the death of the registered owner under Montenegrin succession law.

Montenegrin inheritance law is governed by the Zakon o nasljeđivanju (Inheritance Act). Intestate succession distributes property in a legally defined order: children and spouse are the first class of heirs; parents and siblings if no descendants exist; and more distant relatives thereafter.

A valid will (testament) in Montenegro can alter the intestate distribution, subject to the nužni dio (mandatory share) rule: children and certain other close relatives are entitled to a protected minimum proportion of the estate regardless of the will's terms.

To transfer inherited Montenegrin property into the heirs' names, a rješenje o nasljeđivanju (inheritance decision) must be obtained from the nadležni sud (competent court) or a javni bilježnik where this is permitted. The decision is then registered with the Uprava za nekretnine.

Foreign heirs of Montenegrin property must follow the same legal process as Montenegrin nationals. Apostilled foreign documents (death certificates, marriage certificates, birth certificates proving kinship) are required. A Montenegrin lawyer significantly simplifies cross-border inheritance administration.

Common Mistake: Buying Montenegrin property from heirs without verifying that all heirs identified in the rješenje o nasljeđivanju have signed the ugovor o kupoprodaji. Missing heirs can challenge the sale in court.
Expert Tip: Before purchasing inherited Montenegrin property, instruct your lawyer to obtain the full rješenje o nasljeđivanju and verify that all named heirs are included in the sale agreement. Cross-reference the heirs with the Katastarski Registar entry.

Buyers of inherited Montenegrin property must verify that all heirs have signed the sale agreement. Any heir who was omitted from the inheritance proceedings retains the right to claim their share — a serious risk for buyers who fail to conduct a thorough heir search.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a rješenje o nasljeđivanju in Montenegro?

A rješenje o nasljeđivanju is the Montenegrin court or notarial inheritance decision confirming the heirs' entitlements to a deceased person's estate. It is submitted to the Uprava za nekretnine to transfer property registration from the deceased to the heirs.

What is the nužni dio in Montenegrin inheritance law?

The nužni dio (mandatory share) is the legally protected minimum inheritance right of close relatives — primarily children. Even if a Montenegrin will attempts to disinherit them, they are entitled to at least half of what they would have received under intestate succession.

Can a foreigner inherit Montenegrin property?

Yes. Foreign nationals may inherit Montenegrin property under Montenegrin law on the same basis as Montenegrin citizens. Foreign documents proving kinship must be apostilled and may require translation into Montenegrin by a certified translator.
