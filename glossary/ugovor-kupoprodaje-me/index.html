Ugovor o Kupoprodaji

A ugovor o kupoprodaji (sale and purchase agreement) is the Montenegrin property purchase contract — the written, notarially verified agreement transferring real estate ownership from seller to buyer in Montenegro.

In Montenegro, the transfer of real property requires a written ugovor o kupoprodaji verified by a Montenegrin javni bilježnik (notary public). The notary confirms the parties' identities, verifies the property's cadastral status, and certifies the contract. Since Montenegro's Notary Act, notaries have played a central role in property transactions.

The agreement must identify both parties with their JMBG (identification numbers), provide the precise cadastral description of the property (opština, katastarska parcela), state the agreed purchase price, payment terms, and include a clausula intabulandi authorising the buyer's registration in the Katastarski Registar.

Montenegro levies a porez na promet nepokretnosti (property transfer tax) of 3% of the market value on resale transactions. New builds sold by developers attract 21% PDV (VAT). The tax is administered by the Poreska Uprava Crne Gore (Tax Administration).

EU and EEA citizens may purchase Montenegrin property freely. Non-EEA nationals can also purchase on the basis of reciprocity — most Western countries have reciprocity with Montenegro. Agricultural land has additional restrictions.

Common Mistake: Assuming the signed ugovor o kupoprodaji without notarial verification is legally sufficient in Montenegro. Only a notarially verified contract can be submitted for cadastral registration and legally transfer title.
Expert Tip: Pull a fresh cadastral extract (list nepokretnosti) from the Uprava za nekretnine portal on the morning of the notarial signing. The extract shows the current ownership and all registered encumbrances.

Montenegro's real estate market has grown rapidly, particularly in coastal areas (Boka Kotorska, Budva Riviera). Foreign buyer activity is high, creating demand for bilingual notaries and lawyers. Always verify a Montenegrin notary's registration with the Notarska Komora Crne Gore.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a notary required for Montenegrin property purchases?

Yes. A ugovor o kupoprodaji must be verified by a licensed Montenegrin javni bilježnik to be legally effective for transfer and cadastral registration. Unverified agreements do not transfer legal title.

What is the property transfer tax rate in Montenegro?

The porez na promet nepokretnosti is levied at 3% of the declared market value for resale property. New builds sold by PDV-registered developers attract 21% VAT instead. The buyer typically pays both taxes.

Can non-EU citizens buy property in Montenegro?

Yes, on the basis of reciprocity. Most Western countries have bilateral reciprocity treaties with Montenegro allowing their citizens to purchase Montenegrin real estate on the same terms as Montenegrin nationals, subject to any agricultural land restrictions.
