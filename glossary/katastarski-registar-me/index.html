Katastarski Registar

The Katastarski Registar is Montenegro's cadastral register — the official state database of real property maintained by the Uprava za nekretnine (Real Estate Administration) and recording all ownership rights and encumbrances.

Montenegro's Katastarski Registar (also called the Real Property Register) is administered by the Uprava za nekretnine (Montenegrin Real Estate Administration). It is the definitive record of property ownership and serves as both the physical cadastre and the legal land registry, unified in one database.

Registration in the Katastarski Registar is constitutive — ownership only passes to the buyer upon cadastral entry, not upon signing the ugovor o kupoprodaji. The notary submits the verified contract to the Uprava za nekretnine for registration.

Each property is identified in the Katastarski Registar by its opština (municipality), katastarska parcela (cadastral parcel), and, for apartments, by its object/unit number. These identifiers must be used precisely in all legal documents.

The Uprava za nekretnine operates an online portal (nekretnine.me) allowing free public searches of the register. Buyers can check ownership, registered encumbrances, and property descriptions online before instructing a notary.

Common Mistake: Purchasing Montenegrin property without verifying the list nepokretnosti on the day of signing. Encumbrances registered after the initial due diligence but before signing will appear on a same-day extract but not on an older one.
Expert Tip: Use the Uprava za nekretnine portal (nekretnine.me) to download a list nepokretnosti the morning of the signing appointment. The free online extract is updated in near real-time.

Before purchasing Montenegrin property, obtain a fresh list nepokretnosti (property record extract) from the Uprava za nekretnine. This document shows the seller's registered ownership, any hipoteke (mortgages), zabrane (prohibitions), and registered easements.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Montenegro's property register publicly accessible?

Yes. The Uprava za nekretnine operates a free online portal at nekretnine.me where anyone can search the Katastarski Registar by municipality and parcel number to view ownership information and encumbrances.

What is a list nepokretnosti in Montenegro?

A list nepokretnosti is the official property record extract from Montenegro's Katastarski Registar. It shows the current owner, property description, and all registered encumbrances including mortgages, prohibitions, and easements.

How long does cadastral registration take in Montenegro?

After submission of the notarially verified ugovor o kupoprodaji, registration in the Katastarski Registar typically takes five to fifteen working days depending on the Uprava za nekretnine's workload. An annotation (zabilježba) is placed on the record during this period.
