Građevinska Dozvola

A građevinska dozvola is a Montenegrin building permit — the official authorisation from the nadležni organ (competent authority) permitting construction, reconstruction, or significant alteration of a building.

The građevinska dozvola is required before any new construction or major structural work in Montenegro. It is issued by the nadležni organ — either the local uprava (administration) or a state body depending on the project's scale — after review of the main project documentation (glavni projekat) and compliance with the prostorno-urbanistički plan (spatial planning document).

Montenegro's construction boom, particularly in coastal and ski resort areas, has generated significant demand for građevinska dozvola applications. Developers must hold a valid permit before legally commencing work. Buyers of off-plan properties should verify the permit's existence and scope.

The Uprava za inspekcijske poslove (Inspection Administration) monitors construction compliance. Buildings constructed without a valid građevinska dozvola are subject to demolition orders and regularisation proceedings. Buyers of completed properties should verify that the building has a valid upotrebna dozvola (occupation certificate) as well as the original građevinska dozvola.

For off-plan purchases in Montenegro, the developer should provide a copy of the građevinska dozvola and proof of its registration with the competent authority. Off-plan buyers should hold deposits in escrow until the permit is confirmed as valid.

Common Mistake: Purchasing an off-plan Montenegrin property without verifying the developer holds a valid građevinska dozvola. Without a permit, the construction is illegal and the development may be stopped or demolished before completion.
Expert Tip: Request a certified copy of the građevinska dozvola and verify it directly with the issuing authority before committing to any off-plan Montenegrin purchase. Also check whether the permit has been amended since issue — modifications require supplementary permits.

Montenegro's spatial planning system has evolved significantly, with new urbanism legislation periodically updating requirements. Older permits may have been issued under superseded planning rules — buyers should confirm current compliance status.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an upotrebna dozvola in Montenegro?

An upotrebna dozvola is the occupation certificate issued after construction is completed and inspected as compliant with the građevinska dozvola. Without an upotrebna dozvola, a building cannot be legally occupied and may face difficulties being registered in the Katastarski Registar.

What happens to an unauthorised building in Montenegro?

A building constructed without a valid građevinska dozvola is liable to a demolition order from the Uprava za inspekcijske poslove. Montenegro has some legalisation provisions for pre-existing informal structures, but post-legalisation deadline constructions remain at risk.

Can a foreign buyer obtain a građevinska dozvola in Montenegro?

Yes. Foreign nationals who legally own land in Montenegro can apply for a građevinska dozvola on the same basis as Montenegrin nationals. The application requires an approved main project document (glavni projekat) prepared by a licensed Montenegrin architect.
