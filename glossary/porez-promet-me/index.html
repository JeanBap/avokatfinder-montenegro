Porez na Promet Nepokretnosti

Porez na promet nepokretnosti is the Montenegrin real estate transfer tax of 3% — payable on resale property transactions and assessed by the Poreska Uprava Crne Gore on the declared or market value.

Montenegro levies a 3% transfer tax on resale property purchases. The tax is payable by the buyer and is assessed by the Poreska Uprava Crne Gore (Tax Administration). If the declared purchase price is below the official market reference value, tax is assessed on the higher reference figure.

The transfer tax does not apply to new-build sales by PDV-registered developers — these are subject to 21% PDV (VAT) instead. This makes new-build purchases significantly more expensive in tax terms, a critical consideration for buyers comparing new versus resale properties.

The 3% transfer tax is payable within 15 days of the signed and verified ugovor o kupoprodaji. Late payment attracts interest and penalties. The Uprava za nekretnine will not register the ownership transfer until tax clearance from the Poreska Uprava is confirmed.

Some Montenegrin transfers are exempt from the 3% tax, including transfers between close family members and certain government-programme transactions. Exemption applications must be filed with the Poreska Uprava with supporting documentation.

Common Mistake: Assuming the 3% rate applies to a new-build Montenegrin property. New builds from PDV-registered developers attract 21% PDV — vastly higher than the resale transfer tax. Always determine which regime applies before calculating acquisition costs.
Expert Tip: Pay the porez na promet nepokretnosti within the 15-day window to avoid penalties and delays to cadastral registration. Your Montenegrin lawyer should handle the tax payment simultaneously with the registration application.

Montenegro has periodically amended its real estate tax legislation. Buyers should confirm current rates with a Montenegrin lawyer before committing, as the tax framework may have changed since this guidance was prepared.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who pays the transfer tax in Montenegro?

The buyer is responsible for paying the 3% porez na promet nepokretnosti within 15 days of signing the verified ugovor o kupoprodaji. Failure to pay blocks cadastral registration of the transfer.

What is the VAT rate on new Montenegrin property?

New-build residential property sold by a PDV-registered developer in Montenegro is subject to 21% PDV (VAT). This replaces the 3% transfer tax and significantly increases the acquisition cost compared to resale property.

Can Montenegro's Tax Administration reassess the declared purchase price?

Yes. If the Poreska Uprava considers the declared price below the official market reference value for the property's zone, it will assess the 3% tax on the higher reference figure. Declare the genuine transaction price to avoid this risk.
