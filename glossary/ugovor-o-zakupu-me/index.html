Ugovor o Zakupu Poslovnog Prostora

A ugovor o zakupu poslovnog prostora is a Montenegrin commercial property lease — the contract governing the letting of business premises including offices, retail units, and warehouses.

Commercial property leasing in Montenegro is governed primarily by the Zakon o obligacionim odnosima (Law on Obligations) rather than specific commercial tenancy legislation. This gives parties considerable freedom to negotiate terms, but also means there are fewer statutory protections than in residential letting.

A ugovor o zakupu poslovnog prostora must be in writing and should comprehensively cover the leased premises, zakupnina (rent), term, break options, service charge obligations, VAT treatment, permitted use, maintenance responsibilities, deposit, and reinstatement obligations. Ambiguous or missing clauses are interpreted by reference to general contract law.

VAT (PDV at 21%) applies to commercial property leases in Montenegro where the landlord is a PDV-registered business. Tenants who are themselves PDV-registered can typically recover the input PDV, but non-registered tenants face an irrecoverable additional cost.

For leases exceeding one year, registration in the Katastarski Registar is strongly advisable. A registered commercial lease binds any purchaser of the property — protecting the tenant's right to continue in occupation if the landlord sells. An unregistered lease may not bind a new owner.

Common Mistake: Signing a Montenegrin commercial lease without an express break clause. Commercial leases under Montenegrin law do not imply a right of early termination — a tenant locked into a multi-year lease without a break clause has very limited exit options if the business fails.
Expert Tip: Include a force majeure clause and a rent review mechanism in any Montenegrin commercial lease exceeding one year. Montenegro's tourism-driven economy creates seasonal demand fluctuations that make both clauses commercially important.

Montenegro's growing tourism and hospitality sector has created significant demand for commercial leasing, particularly in coastal areas. Market rents in prime Budva and Kotor locations can be substantial, and professional legal advice on lease terms is essential before committing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is VAT payable on Montenegrin commercial property leases?

Yes. Where the landlord is a PDV-registered business, 21% PDV applies to the zakupnina. PDV-registered tenants can reclaim the input tax. Non-registered tenants bear the PDV as an irrecoverable cost — a significant factor in rent negotiations.

Does a Montenegrin commercial lease survive the sale of the property?

A registered ugovor o zakupu poslovnog prostora (registered in the Katastarski Registar) binds any new owner. An unregistered commercial lease may not be enforceable against a purchaser who acquires without notice of the tenancy.

How is a Montenegrin commercial lease terminated?

Termination is governed by the lease agreement itself. In the absence of an express termination right, a party can only exit the lease by mutual agreement or on the grounds provided by the Zakon o obligacionim odnosima — such as material breach by the other party.
