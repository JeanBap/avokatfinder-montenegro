Property Rights

Legal entitlements to own, use, transfer, and exclude others from property, including real estate and personal property.

Definition

Property rights in Montenegro establish the legal entitlements that persons and entities have over property, including real estate, movable goods, and intangible assets. These rights form the foundation of economic activity and personal autonomy. The owner of property has the right to use the property, derive income from it, transfer it to others, and exclude others from using it. However, property rights are not absolute and must be exercised in accordance with law and respect for the rights of others.

Real property in Montenegro includes land and buildings permanently attached to land. Ownership of real property is established through registration in the cadastre, the public register of property. The cadastre provides notice of ownership and protects against competing claims. Transfer of real property must be recorded in the cadastre to be effective against third parties. Ownership can be encumbered by mortgages, easements, and other rights in favor of others, which are also registered in the cadastre.

Movable property includes personal goods such as vehicles, equipment, and consumer goods. Ownership of movable property is typically established through possession and bills of sale. Unlike real property, movable property is not required to be registered in a public register, though registration systems exist for some types of movable property such as vehicles. Ownership of movable property can be transferred through sale, gift, or inheritance.

Owners have the right to use their property as they see fit, subject to limitations imposed by law, regulations, and the rights of neighbors. Zoning laws, environmental regulations, and building codes limit how property can be used. Owners must not cause nuisance to neighbors or use property in ways that are dangerous or illegal. Disputes between neighbors over property use are resolved through courts applying principles of reasonable use and balancing of interests.

Intellectual property rights protect creations of the mind, including patents, copyrights, trademarks, and trade secrets. These rights give creators exclusive control over their creations and the right to receive compensation for their use. Intellectual property is protected for limited periods, after which creations may enter the public domain. Enforcement of intellectual property rights requires registration and vigilant monitoring for infringement.

Key Facts

  • Property rights include the right to use, derive income from, transfer, and exclude others from property.
  • Real property ownership is established and protected through registration in the cadastre.
  • Movable property is owned through possession and established through bills of sale.
  • Property use is limited by zoning, environmental law, building codes, and duties to neighbors.
  • Intellectual property protects creations of the mind for limited periods.

Common Mistake

Property owners assume they can use property as they wish without considering zoning restrictions and neighbor rights.

Expert Tip

Before acquiring property or making significant improvements, verify zoning restrictions and applicable regulations.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is ownership of real property established in Montenegro?

Ownership is established through registration in the cadastre, the public register of property rights.

Can property rights be limited?

Yes, property rights can be limited by law, regulations, mortgages, easements, and duties to neighbors and the public.

What is intellectual property?

Intellectual property includes patents, copyrights, trademarks, and trade secrets that protect creations of the mind.
