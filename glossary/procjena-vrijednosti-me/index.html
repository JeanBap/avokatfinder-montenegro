Procjena Vrijednosti Nekretnine

Procjena vrijednosti nekretnine is the Montenegrin property valuation — an independent professional assessment of a property's market value used for mortgage lending, tax, and transaction purposes.

A procjena vrijednosti (property valuation) in Montenegro is typically conducted by a licensed ovlašteni procjenitelj (certified appraiser) for mortgage applications, probate, tax assessments, and contested disputes. Banks require an independent procjena before approving a hipoteka loan.

Montenegrin certified appraisers are regulated and must be licensed by the relevant professional body. Appraisal fees are typically 0.1–0.3% of the assessed value, with minimums set by the professional organisation. Lenders often maintain a panel of approved appraisers.

The procjena is based on comparative sales data, property condition, location, and market trends. In Montenegro's dynamic coastal market, values can shift significantly between tourist seasons, making the timing of the procjena relevant to both parties.

For tax purposes, the Poreska Uprava uses its own reference values (tržišna vrijednost) rather than relying on private procjena. If the declared purchase price falls below the Poreska Uprava's reference value, the transfer tax is assessed on the higher reference figure.

Common Mistake: Relying solely on the seller's own estimate or an estate agent's opinion as a substitute for a formal procjena. Banks require an independent certified appraisal for mortgage purposes — and buyers benefit from one for their own protection regardless.
Expert Tip: Commission the procjena from a certified appraiser on the lender's approved panel to avoid a second valuation being required. Banks will only accept appraisals from appraisers they have vetted and approved.

Off-plan buyers in Montenegro may struggle to obtain a reliable procjena before completion since the property does not yet exist. Lenders typically base pre-completion valuations on the developer's plans and comparable completed sales, then conduct a full appraisal on the completed unit.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can conduct a property valuation in Montenegro?

Only an ovlašteni procjenitelj (certified appraiser) licensed by the relevant Montenegrin professional body can produce a legally recognised procjena vrijednosti. Bank mortgage panels require appraisers to be pre-approved by the lending institution.

Does Montenegro's Tax Administration accept private valuations for transfer tax purposes?

No. The Poreska Uprava uses its own tržišna vrijednost (market reference values) by zone and property type. If the declared price is below the Poreska Uprava's reference, the 3% transfer tax is assessed on the higher figure regardless of any private valuation.

How is a Montenegrin property valuation used in probate?

In Montenegrin inheritance proceedings, a procjena vrijednosti is used to establish the value of real estate in the estate for distribution among heirs and for calculating any applicable inheritance taxes. The court or notary may require a certified procjena as part of the rješenje o nasljeđivanju process.
