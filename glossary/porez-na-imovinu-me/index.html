Porez na Imovinu

Porez na imovinu is the Montenegrin annual property tax — a recurring levy on real estate ownership paid each year to the local municipality based on the property's official assessed value.

Montenegro levies an annual porez na imovinu (property tax) on all real estate — residential, commercial, and land. The tax is administered by and payable to the local opština (municipality) where the property is situated. Both Montenegrin nationals and foreign property owners are liable.

The tax base is the official market value of the property as assessed by the opština for tax purposes. Rates vary by municipality and property category but are generally modest — typically 0.1–1% of assessed value per annum for residential property. Coastal municipalities such as Budva and Kotor often have higher rates reflecting higher property values.

Non-resident property owners in Montenegro receive tax assessment notices at the property address. Many non-residents appoint a local agent (punomoćnik) to receive notices, make payments, and manage compliance. Failure to pay results in penalties and interest, and unpaid tax can become a charge on the property.

At the point of purchasing Montenegrin property, buyers should request confirmation from the seller that all outstanding porez na imovinu has been paid. An unpaid tax liability can transfer to the new owner's obligation or result in a registered charge that complicates subsequent transactions.

Common Mistake: Purchasing Montenegrin property without requesting a confirmation of paid taxes from the seller. Unpaid annual property tax can become the new owner's liability or result in a municipal charge registered against the property.
Expert Tip: Appoint a local Montenegrin property manager or accountant to receive and pay annual porez na imovinu assessments on your behalf, especially if you are a non-resident. This prevents missed payments and accumulating penalties.

Budget the annual porez na imovinu as a recurring holding cost when assessing the investment case for Montenegrin real estate. In coastal areas, the combination of property tax and building maintenance fund contributions represents a meaningful annual outlay.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who pays the annual property tax in Montenegro?

All owners of Montenegrin real estate — including foreign nationals — are liable for the annual porez na imovinu payable to the local opština. There is no exemption for non-residents.

What is the Montenegro property tax rate?

The rate varies by municipality and property type. For residential property, rates typically range from 0.1% to 1% of the official assessed value per annum. Coastal municipalities generally apply higher rates reflecting higher property values.

Can unpaid Montenegrin property tax be inherited by a buyer?

Yes. Unpaid porez na imovinu can become a charge on the property or result in a municipal lien. Buyers should request a tax clearance certificate from the local opština before completing any Montenegrin property purchase.
