Zapljena Nekretnine

Zapljena nekretnine is a Montenegrin judicial attachment (seizure order) over real property — a court-ordered measure prohibiting the sale, transfer, or further encumbrance of a property as part of enforcement or litigation proceedings.

A zapljena nekretnine (property attachment) is issued by a Montenegrin court as part of enforcement proceedings (izvršni postupak) or as an interim measure in civil litigation. Once issued, the zapljena is registered in the Katastarski Registar as a notation, immediately visible to any searcher of the property's record.

The zapljena prevents the owner from selling the property, granting new mortgages, or otherwise dealing with it until the attachment is lifted. Buyers who discover a zapljena on a property's Katastarski Registar entry must not proceed until the attachment is fully resolved and lifted.

An attachment can arise from a debt owed by the property owner to a creditor, from a tax debt owed to the state, from enforcement of a court judgment, or from bankruptcy proceedings. The cause of the attachment determines what is required to lift it.

The zapljena is lifted once the underlying debt is paid or the litigation is resolved, and the court issues an order for its removal. The order is submitted to the Uprava za nekretnine for deregistration from the Katastarski Registar.

Common Mistake: Proceeding with a Montenegrin property purchase where a zapljena is registered in the Katastarski Registar. A zapljena must be fully lifted before ownership can be transferred — never proceed on the basis of the seller's assurance that it 'will be resolved'.
Expert Tip: Always pull a fresh list nepokretnosti extract from the Uprava za nekretnine portal before signing — zapljene can be registered quickly and a week-old extract may miss an attachment issued recently against the seller.

Montenegrin court proceedings can be slow. Buyers who discover a zapljena should budget for potentially lengthy delays while the attachment is resolved — and consider whether to withdraw from the transaction if resolution is not certain within an acceptable timeframe.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a zapljena in Montenegrin property law?

Zapljena nekretnine is a judicial attachment order prohibiting the owner from dealing with a property. It is registered in the Katastarski Registar and is immediately visible to anyone searching the property's record.

How is a Montenegrin property zapljena lifted?

The zapljena is lifted once the underlying obligation (debt, judgment, or litigation) is resolved and the court issues an order for deregistration. This order is submitted to the Uprava za nekretnine, which removes the notation from the Katastarski Registar.

Can I buy Montenegrin property with a zapljena registered?

Not while the zapljena remains active — the Uprava za nekretnine will reject a registration application for a transfer of property subject to an active zapljena. The attachment must be fully lifted and deregistered before a clean transfer can be completed.
