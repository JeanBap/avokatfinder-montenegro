Etažna Svojina

Etažna svojina is Montenegrin condominium ownership — a form of property ownership where each owner holds exclusive title to an individual unit plus a proportional undivided share in the building's common areas.

Etažna svojina is the standard ownership structure for apartments and commercial units in Montenegrin multi-unit buildings. Governed by the Zakon o svojinsko-pravnim odnosima (Property Rights Act), it grants each owner exclusive rights over their unit while mandating shared responsibility for common areas (zajednički dijelovi).

Common areas in an etažna svojina building include staircases, lifts, roof, external walls, and the land beneath the building. These cannot be individually sold but each owner's proportional share forms part of their overall property right — visible in their cadastral entry.

Etažna svojina owners are collectively responsible for building maintenance. They typically appoint an upravnik (building manager) and contribute to a zajednički fond (common fund / reserve fund) for maintenance costs. Major decisions require a majority vote of the etažna svojina community.

For buyers of Montenegrin apartments, the reserve fund balance and maintenance history are important due diligence items. An underfunded common fund signals that major future expenditure will fall on current owners through special assessments.

Common Mistake: Overlooking the common fund (zajednički fond) balance when buying a Montenegrin apartment. An underfunded reserve means the new owner will face immediate special assessments for deferred maintenance — particularly prevalent in older coastal complexes.
Expert Tip: Request the last three years of etažna svojina community accounts and the current maintenance plan from the building manager (upravnik) before signing the purchase contract. This reveals the financial health of the building.

Montenegro's coastal property market features many etažna svojina complexes. Foreign buyers should ensure they understand the zajednički fond obligations and the governance structure before purchasing, as poorly managed buildings can result in significant unplanned costs.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is zajednički fond in Montenegrin condominium law?

Zajednički fond (common fund or reserve fund) is the collective maintenance reserve of a Montenegrin etažna svojina community. All owners contribute monthly proportional amounts to fund ongoing maintenance and future capital expenditures on common areas.

Who manages a Montenegrin etažna svojina building?

An upravnik (building manager) is appointed by the etažna svojina community. The upravnik handles day-to-day maintenance, accounts, and compliance. Major decisions — such as significant repairs or changes to the building rules — require a majority owner vote.

Can I sell a Montenegrin apartment without the co-owners' consent?

Yes. An individual etažna svojina unit can be sold without other owners' consent. However, any first refusal rights held by co-owners (in the case of shared ownership within a unit) must be respected. The common areas cannot be separately sold.
